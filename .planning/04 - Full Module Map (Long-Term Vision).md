# 4. Full Module Map (Long-Term Vision)

## Purpose of This Map

This document captures the **complete long-term ambition** for Life OS. It is a map of every life domain and capability that could eventually live inside the system—a personal operating system that spans tasks, calendar, finances, health, relationships, knowledge, home, career, travel, administration, growth, and family.

This map is not a build plan. It is a **north star**. The product will grow toward it incrementally, adding domains only after the core daily planning loop has proven its value and retention. Each new domain must answer one question before it is built: *Does adding this information make the user’s daily decision clearer, not more complex?*

The modules are organized into **Spaces**—high-level life domains—each containing feature‑rich modules. Every module is designed to be deep on its own but never feel like a separate application, because all are connected through the **Life Graph**, the **universal command bar**, and the **capacity‑aware planning surface**.

---

## 1. Plan & Execute (The Core Spine)

This Space is the heart of Life OS. It contains the planning engine that sits on top of all other domains, and the essential tools for managing work and time.

- **Inbox / Capture** – Universal capture for anything: tasks, ideas, notes, reminders, images, voice memos, email forwards, and shared links.
- **Task Manager** – Full task management with subtasks, dependencies, priorities, durations, deadlines, recurrence, statuses, energy/context tags, batch actions, and defer/split/reschedule capabilities.
- **Calendar & Time Blocking** – External calendar sync (eventually read/write), native focus blocks, time boxing, scheduling links, commute/transition time, and buffer slots.
- **Daily Planner** – The primary Today surface: user-chosen outcomes, a combined timeline of events and focus blocks, a flexible next-action queue, and dynamic, explainable rescheduling.
- **Weekly / Monthly / Quarterly / Annual Reviews** – Structured reflection cycles, goal checkpoints, and automated summaries.
- **Goals & OKRs** – Long-term aspirations, milestones, measurable key results, and alignment with projects.
- **Routines & Habits** – Repeating habits with flexible schedules, streak tracking, and forgiveness logic.
- **Pomodoro / Focus Timer** – Built-in timed focus sessions linked to tasks, with optional flow-state tracking.
- **Decision Journal** – A dedicated log for important decisions, rationale, expected outcomes, and later review.

## 2. Finance

Full personal finance management, eventually with secure account aggregation and transaction data. This Space is subject to the highest privacy standards and will reside in the **Vault Zone** when implemented.

- **Banking & Credit Card Sync** – Secure aggregation (via Plaid or similar), transaction feeds, and real-time balances.
- **Budgeting** – Category budgets, envelope system, rollovers, alerts, and “safe-to-spend” calculations.
- **Bill Management** – Recurring bill tracking, due-date reminders, links to tasks, and cash-flow forecasting.
- **Income Tracking** – Multiple income streams, irregular income, and tax estimates.
- **Expense Tracking** – Manual and auto-categorized spending, receipt scanning (OCR), and mileage.
- **Saving Goals & Funds** – Emergency fund, vacation fund, and goal jars with visual progress.
- **Debt Payoff Planner** – Snowball/avalanche strategies and payoff simulations.
- **Investment Portfolio** – Holdings view, performance tracking, and asset allocation (read-only).
- **Net Worth Dashboard** – Automatic aggregation of assets and liabilities over time.
- **Tax Preparation** – Deductible-expense tagging, document storage, and quarterly tax estimates.
- **Subscriptions Tracker** – All recurring subscriptions, cost per period, and cancellation reminders.
- **Financial Health Score** – A simple, explainable financial wellness indicator.

## 3. Health & Wellness

A complete personal health record, fitness companion, and mental wellness tool. Due to the sensitivity of health data, this Space will be part of the **Vault Zone** with client‑side encryption.

- **Health Metrics** – Manual and synced (HealthKit, wearables) tracking of weight, blood pressure, heart rate, glucose, and more.
- **Symptom & Pain Log** – Structured tracking for chronic conditions and doctor visits.
- **Medication Manager** – Dose schedules, refill reminders, and basic interaction warnings.
- **Doctor / Appointment Tracking** – Upcoming medical appointments, visit notes, and follow‑up tasks.
- **Lab Results & Documents** – Secure storage for PDFs, images, reference ranges, and trend charts.
- **Fitness & Workout Planner** – Exercise library, workout plans, set/reps logging, progressive overload, and integration with Apple Health/Google Fit.
- **Nutrition & Meal Planning** – Food diary, macronutrient goals, meal plans, recipe storage, and grocery list generation.
- **Sleep Tracking** – Manual or synced sleep duration/quality, sleep debt calculation, and bedtime reminders.
- **Mental Health & Mood** – Daily mood check-ins, journaling prompts, therapy notes, and CBT exercises.
- **Menstrual Cycle / Fertility** – Period tracking, symptom correlation, and predictions.

## 4. Relationships & People

A personal CRM for every meaningful human connection, not just professional contacts.

- **Contact Book** – Rich contact profiles with notes, tags (family, friend, work), and relationship-strength indicators.
- **Interaction Log** – Automatic and manual logging of calls, meetings, and messages; reminders to reach out on a desired cadence.
- **Important Dates** – Birthdays, anniversaries, and date-specific gift ideas with automated reminders.
- **Gift Manager** – Ideas, purchased status, budget, size/preferences, and holiday lists.
- **Networking Tracker** – Professional contacts, context of meeting, and follow‑up sequences.
- **Household / Family Roster** – Shared contact details, roles, and pet information (for future collaboration features).
- **Event Attendee Tracker** – Guest lists, RSVPs, and dietary needs for hosted events.

## How Modules Coexist Without Fragmenting the Experience

A long list of modules is exactly what makes a super app feel heavy. Life OS avoids this trap through a set of architectural and design commitments:

1. **The Life Graph** – Every entity (task, event, financial transaction, contact, note, health metric) is a node that can link to any other node without duplication. A bill is also a task; a doctor’s appointment links to a contact, a health metric, and a follow-up task. The graph dissolves module boundaries.

2. **The Command Bar** – A single, universal input surface for capture, search, and action. “Pay Amex” creates or finds the bill task and links it to the finance space. “Log a 5k run” adds a fitness entry. No module-first navigation is required.

3. **The Today / Plan Surface** – All modules feed constraints and items into a single, capacity-aware daily plan. Health reminders, bill due dates, task deadlines, maintenance chores, and personal growth habits all appear as part of one unified queue, answering only “What should I do now?”

4. **Progressive Disclosure & Contextual Workspaces** – When a user wants to dive deep into budgeting, they can open a full finance workspace. But it still feels like the same app because the design language, interactions, and ability to link to anything else remain identical. The workspace is simply a focused view of a slice of the graph, not a separate mini-app.

5. **Consistent Object Actions** – Every actionable entity (task, bill, event, contact) can be completed, deferred, rescheduled, linked, or archived using the same gestures and commands throughout the system, regardless of which domain it originates from.
